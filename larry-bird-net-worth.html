Larry Bird | Career

As a player

To begin with, Larry started his early childhood basketball career playing for Springs Valley High School and managed to register an average of 31 ppm, 21 rebounds, and 4 assists.

After his lauded career at school, he started playing college basketball for Indiana Hoosiers and managed to score an average of 30.3 points, 13.3 rebounds, and 4.6 assists.

Meanwhile, he started his professional basketball career playing for Boston Celtics after being picked as 6th overall in the 1978 NBA Draft.

With no surprise at all, he was immediately impressed with his gameplay and made Celtics one of the contenders for the title in his rookie year.

Notably, he had a wonderful 1981-82 season where he managed to win the NBA All-Star Game MVP Award for scoring 19 points in All-Star Game.

In addition, in 1987, Larry could not save his team from back-to-back 6 defeats against the Lakers, which cost his team to lose Eastern Conference Finals.

Interestingly, Larry registered the highest stats of his career in the later stages, where he managed to produce 34 points.

Further,  Larry produced healthy stats in his final three years at Celtics, where he averaged over 20 points, 9 rebounds, and 7 assists per game.

Finally, in 1992, Larry decided to retire from his professional basketball career.

And, following his departure, the Boston Celtics decided to retire the number 33 jersey permanently.
