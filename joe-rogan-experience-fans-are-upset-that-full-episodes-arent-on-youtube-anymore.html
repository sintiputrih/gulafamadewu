Joe Rogan Experience Fans Are Upset That Full Episodes Arent On YouTube Anymore

 Posted on March 17, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  402 words  |  Aldo Pusey

The Joe Rogan Experience has undergone several changes since the MMA commentator signed his 2020 deal with Spotify. Rogan's fans watch his videos with no filter in mind and frequently voice their issues with particular episodes or production issues. This time around, they blame Spotify for not releasing full video episodes on Rogan's YouTube channel.

Full Episodes V. Clips

Rogan posted a clip from his interview with comedian Michael Kosta. His full podcast episodes usually gain over a million views in a short amount of time. This twelve-minute clip has now nearly reached a quarter of a million views, but fans are upset with the video's format.

One frequent podcast listener wrote, "It’s sad. I literally ONLY watch the clips now," and another agreed, "The sad part is Spotify doesn’t have podcasts in some countries, thus there is no way for me to watch the JRE anymore."

Others noted the fact that Rogan's fanbase loved their ability to comment and discuss full episodes. That was part of his pull, the unscripted honesty. While the music-focused platform is improving its accessibility, listeners should not have to find topsy turvy ways on the web to make Spotify readily available.

Changes For The Better?

Episode #1567 featuring Donnell Rawlings and Dave Chappelle was the last full episode posted on the podcast's YouTube. That's from about a month ago. Since then, a majority of the clips that are cut specifically for YouTube are only around five minutes long. Once in a while, subscribers are graced with a fifteen-minute video.

This, however, is a drastic change compared to a three-hour episode that allows fans to become an active part of the "experience."

Fans are also wary of Spotify's reasoning behind the new routine. They want to improve viewership on their app considering the multi-million dollar deal they made with Rogan.

Does online traction require diluting the grounded ambiance Rogan's nurtured with the podcast?

Another fan clarified that this shift was inevitable and was a part of the Spotify transition, "Joe Rogan talked at the very beginning of the spotify deal that he would still have his JRE Clips page but all full interviews will be posted full on spotify and then their clips page a couple days later. It was all apart of the deal."
