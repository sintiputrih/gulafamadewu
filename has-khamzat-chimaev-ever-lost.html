Has Khamzat Chimaev ever lost?

 Posted on August 25, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  616 words  |  Jenniffer Sheldon

Khamzat Chimaev, a rising star in the world of mixed martial arts (MMA), has garnered significant attention due to his impressive unbeaten record. With an incredible streak of victories, many fans and experts have wondered if Chimaev has ever tasted defeat. In this article, we will explore Chimaev's career, his accomplishments, and whether he has indeed suffered any losses.

Khamzat Chimaev: The Rising Star

Born on May 1, 1994, in Chechnya, Russia, Khamzat Chimaev is a Swedish professional MMA fighter of Chechen descent. He started his professional career in 2018 and quickly gained attention for his dominant performances in the cage. Chimaev is known for his aggressive style, exceptional grappling skills, and relentless ground and pound.

A Flawless Record

As of this writing, Khamzat Chimaev boasts an impressive professional record of 9 wins and no losses. Out of his nine victories, he has achieved eight by way of stoppage, including three knockouts and five submissions. His ability to finish fights has made him a fan favorite and has caught the attention of MMA promotions worldwide.

Chimaev's Dominant UFC Debut

Chimaev made his UFC debut on July 15, 2020, at UFC Fight Island 1. He faced off against John Phillips in a middleweight bout. Chimaev showcased his dominance by securing a second-round submission victory via D'Arce choke. This impressive win caught the attention of the MMA community and set the stage for what would become a meteoric rise.

A Record-Breaking Streak

Following his victorious UFC debut, Khamzat Chimaev wasted no time in making a name for himself. He quickly booked his next fight, this time in the welterweight division, against Rhys McKee on July 25, 2020, at UFC Fight Night 173. Chimaev once again displayed his dominance, securing a first-round TKO victory just 3:09 into the fight. This win marked the fastest turnaround between wins in modern UFC history, a record previously held by Nate Diaz.

A Double Victory in 10 Days

In his quest to leave no doubt about his abilities, Khamzat Chimaev accepted another fight just ten days after his second UFC victory. On September 19, 2020, at UFC Fight Night 178, Chimaev faced Gerald Meerschaert in a middleweight bout. Chimaev demonstrated his superiority once again, delivering a devastating knockout just 17 seconds into the first round. This victory solidified Chimaev's status as a rising star and a force to be reckoned with in the UFC.

The Battle with COVID-19

While Khamzat Chimaev's rise to stardom seemed unstoppable, he faced a significant setback in his career. In December 2020, Chimaev contracted COVID-19, which forced him to withdraw from his scheduled fight against Leon Edwards. This unfortunate turn of events put a temporary halt to his momentum and left fans wondering when they would see him back in action.

Return to the Octagon

After a prolonged absence from the cage due to his battle with COVID-19, Khamzat Chimaev returned to action on July 25, 2021, at UFC on ESPN 14. He faced Li Jingliang in a welterweight bout. Chimaev continued to impress, securing a first-round submission victory via a guillotine choke. This win solidified his position as a top contender in the welterweight division and reignited the buzz surrounding his undefeated record.

Khamzat Chimaev has yet to taste defeat in his professional MMA career. With an unbeaten record of 9-0, he has showcased his dominance and remarkable skill set in the UFC. Chimaev's aggressive style, exceptional grappling, and ability to finish fights have propelled him to the top of the welterweight division. While the future is uncertain, it is clear that Khamzat Chimaev is a force to be reckoned with in the world of MMA, and fans eagerly await his next appearance in the octagon.
