Michael Zimmer, born June 5, 1956, is an American football coach who currently serves as the head coach of the National Football League’s Minnesota Vikings (NFL). He formerly worked as the Cincinnati Bengals’, Atlanta Falcons’, and Dallas Cowboys defensive coordinator.

Zimmer was a standout multi-sport athlete in high school, earning all-conference accolades in football (1972–73), baseball, and wrestling at Lockport Township High School in Lockport, Illinois.

Mick Zimmer attended Illinois State University and majored in physical education. He started at quarterback in 1974, but redshirted the next year and converted to linebacker in 1976 after fracturing his thumb. That same year, a neck injury terminated his playing career prematurely.

His collegiate colleagues were affected positively by his continual “up” attitude, particularly during scorching August pre-season double exercises. In 1974, he backed up quarterback Bob Lopez, who went on to establish several of Illinois State’s passing records.
